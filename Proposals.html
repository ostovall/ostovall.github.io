Pawsome Cat Toy: The Remote-Control Cat Toy

by Olivia Stovall

Project Concept / Motivation

I want to build an interactive, remote-control cat toy that allows a pet owner to engage their cat through movement, light, and sound. The goal is to create a low-cost, Arduino-based product that can be controlled wirelessly to entertain indoor cats. The system combines motor motion, LED attraction, and simple obstacle detection to make playtime autonomous or remotely guided.

Anticipated Bill of Materials

  • Arduino UNO R3 (Elegoo Most Complete Starter Kit)
  • Bluetooth or IR receiver module (for remote control input)
  • DC motors or servo motors (for wheel or tail motion)
  • Ultrasonic sensor (for obstacle avoidance)
  • RGB LED (for visual attraction to cats)
  • Buzzer (for optional sound feedback)
  • Breadboard and jumper wires (for connections)
  • 9V battery and holder (for power)
  • Lightweight plastic or 3D-printed chassis (cat-safe housing)

Timeline and Contingency Plans

  • Week 1: Design concept and schematic; verify Bluetooth communication.
  • Week 2: Assemble prototype with motors, LEDs, and sensors; begin testing remote control.
  • Week 3: Integrate obstacle detection and safety logic.
  • Week 4: Assemble final prototype into toy chassis and test with real cat interactions.
  • Plan B: If Bluetooth communication fails, switch to IR remote or pre-programmed motion patterns.

Additional Notes

The project demonstrates integration of multiple electronics concepts—sensor input, motor output, and wireless control—into a single interactive prototype. It’s designed to showcase both creativity and technical ability, emphasizing practical product design and playful interaction for pets.
